About the Camp

A beloved Bay Area tradition now in its 13th year — connecting Filipino-American youth to their roots through hands-on, immersive cultural learning.

More Than a Summer Camp

Presented by UPAASF · Serving the San Francisco Bay Area since 2013

🌟 Mission
To inspire Filipino-American youth to embrace their cultural heritage through immersive educational experiences that promote cultural pride, community connection, and a deeper understanding of Filipino traditions, values, and history.
🎯 Goal
To provide Filipino-American youth with an engaging and supportive environment where they can learn, experience, and celebrate Filipino culture while building pride in their identity and strengthening connections with their community.
🤝 In partnership with St. Raymond's Church and El Camino High School — Bay Area
📸 Camp 2025

Our 2025 campers and volunteer facilitators — all smiles after an amazing week!

Four Core Pillars

Every activity, lesson, and experience at camp is guided by these four principles.

🌺

Cultural Discovery

Introducing youth to Filipino language, arts, food, history, and traditions through hands-on, participatory learning.

🦅

Identity & Pride

Helping Filipino-American youth develop confidence and pride in their heritage and cultural identity.

🤝

Community & Belonging

Creating a supportive environment where youth build friendships, teamwork, and lasting connections with the Fil-Am community.

⭐

Leadership & Service

Encouraging youth to become future leaders who carry forward Filipino culture and contribute positively to their communities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything families want to know before signing up.

Who can attend the Summer Camp? +

The camp is open to Filipino-American youth ages 7–15 in the San Francisco Bay Area. No prior knowledge of Filipino language or culture is required — just curiosity and enthusiasm! All skill levels are warmly welcome.

How much does it cost to attend? +

Tuition is $350 per camper for the full week-long program. This covers teaching materials, instructors' honoraria, camp assistance, a fun field trip (including transportation), and a graduation celebration for campers and families.

Scholarships are available upon request — please contact our camp team to learn more. We believe no child should miss out due to financial constraints.

What does a typical camp day look like? +

Camp runs Monday–Friday, 9:00 AM to 3:30 PM. Each day is packed with immersive classes and activities — from Tagalog language and Filipino history in the morning to dance, arts & crafts, cooking, and martial arts in the afternoon. The week also includes a special field trip, usually to the Philippine Consulate in San Francisco, and closes with a graduation ceremony for campers and families!

Who are the instructors? +

All classes are led by respected Filipino educators, artists, and cultural practitioners from the Bay Area. UPAASF carefully selects instructors who are passionate about Filipino heritage and skilled at engaging with young learners.

Can parents and grandparents get involved? +

Absolutely! Family participation is a big part of what makes this camp special. Parents and grandparents are encouraged to attend the closing graduation ceremony and are welcome to volunteer as camp assistants. We also invite the whole family to the field trip day — it's a wonderful multi-generational experience.

Where will the 2026 camp be held? +
  • Peninsula (South San Francisco)
    El Camino High School — July 13 – July 17, 2026
  • East Bay (Dublin, CA)
    St. Raymond's Church — July 20 – July 24, 2026

Please note: While these dates and locations are currently scheduled, they remain pending final confirmation.

My child isn't Filipino — can they still attend? +

The camp is primarily designed for Filipino-American youth, but we warmly welcome any child who is curious about and respectful of Filipino culture. The more diverse perspectives in the room, the richer the learning experience for everyone!
