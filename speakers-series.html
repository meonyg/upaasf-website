Where Filipino Voices Lead the Conversation

"Education, inspiration, and connection — one forum at a time."

The UPAASF Speakers' Series — branded as UPConnect — is an ongoing quarterly program that brings together professionals, academics, and community leaders for engaging conversations on topics that matter most to the Filipino and Filipino-American community.

From the frontlines of health and longevity science to the realities of building a small business, from the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence to the art of forging cross-university networks across the Filipino diaspora, UP Connect provides a platform where Filipino voices lead — and the whole community gains.

Every session is free, open to the community, and designed to foster meaningful dialogue, lasting connections, and the kind of insights that can change a career, a perspective, or a life.

Format: In-person forums with panelists and Q&A, held quarterly at the Philippine Consulate General in San Francisco and community venues across the Bay Area.

🤝 In partnership with the Philippine Consulate General, SF 🏛️ In collaboration with Sentro Filipino (SF Filipino Cultural Center)

What We Talk About

Each UPConnect session is anchored in one of six topic domains — all chosen for their direct relevance to the Filipino and Filipino-American experience.

🏥

Health & Longevity

Insights on healthy aging, mental wellness, medical breakthroughs, and living a longer, fuller life.

💼

Business & Entrepreneurship

Pathways to business ownership, financial literacy, exit strategies, and sustaining growth.

💻

Technology & Innovation

From AI and Silicon Valley trends to the tech opportunities shaping Filipino careers and communities.

💰

Finance & Career

Personal finance, investment strategies, career development, and professional growth in the Bay Area.

🌏

Community & Culture

Filipino-American identity, cultural preservation, advocacy, and cross-diaspora collaboration.

🎓

Education & Leadership

Lifelong learning, mentorship, civic leadership, and empowering the next generation of Filipinos.

How Each Session Works

Every UPConnect — Speakers' Series event follows a consistent format designed to maximize learning, engagement, and meaningful connection among attendees.

1

Reception & Networking

Arrive early for light refreshments and informal networking with fellow alumni, community members, and speakers.

2

Expert Presentations

Two or more featured speakers — professionals, academics, or community leaders — share insights on the session theme.

3

Panel Discussion

Speakers engage in moderated conversation, exploring diverse perspectives and the Filipino-American lens on the topic.

4

Open Q&A & Connections

Attendees ask questions directly, then continue conversations informally in an open networking close.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before attending your first UP Connect event.

Who can attend UP Connect events? +
All UP Connect events are free and open to the broader Filipino and Filipino-American community — not just UP alumni or UPAASF members. We welcome anyone who is curious, eager to learn, and interested in connecting with our community. UPAASF members may receive priority registration for popular events.
How do I find out about upcoming events? +
The best ways to stay informed: (1) check our Events page at upaasf.org/events, (2) follow us on Instagram @upaa_sf and Facebook, (3) subscribe to our monthly newsletter, or (4) become a UPAASF member and receive event notifications directly. We typically announce each session 4–6 weeks in advance.
Where are events held? +
Most UP Connect events are held at Kalayaan Hall, Philippine Consulate General, 447 Sutter St, San Francisco, CA, in partnership with the Consulate. Some sessions may also be held at community venues across the Bay Area or offered in a hybrid in-person/virtual format. Exact location details are always confirmed in the event announcement.
Is there a cost to attend? +
UP Connect events are free of charge. UPAASF is committed to making professional and community development accessible to all. Some sessions include a light reception or networking refreshments. While attendance is free, RSVP is typically required as space is limited at the Consulate venue.
Can I suggest a topic or speaker for a future session? +
Absolutely — and we encourage it! UP Connect's programming is shaped by what matters to our community. Use the Contact page to submit a topic suggestion or speaker recommendation. If you are a professional with expertise to share, we'd love to hear from you directly.
Do I need to be a UP alumnus to attend or speak? +
No UP degree is required to attend — the series is open to the Filipino and Filipino-American community broadly. For speakers and panelists, we prioritize voices who bring relevant expertise and a connection to the Filipino diaspora experience, whether or not they are UP alumni. Our goal is to amplify the best Filipino voices on each topic.
