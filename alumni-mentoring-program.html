Connecting Iskolar ng Bayan Across Generations

"Guiding today's students to become tomorrow's leaders"

The UPAASF Alumni Mentoring Program pairs UP students in their graduating year with seasoned alumni professionals across the San Francisco Bay Area. Mentors bring their industry expertise, personal networks, and lived experience to help mentees navigate career decisions, build professional skills, and unlock opportunities in one of the world's most dynamic job markets.

Program Cycle: Each cohort runs for six months, with a new cycle launching every November. Pairs are carefully matched based on career interests, industries, and mentee goals.

A First-of-Its-Kind Initiative

Launched in November 2023, this program is the first of its kind among UP alumni chapters worldwide. How It Works

A structured, six-month journey from application to graduation — with support every step of the way.

1

Apply

Submit your application sharing your background, career interests, and what you're hoping to gain from mentorship.

2

Get Matched

Our team carefully reviews applications and matches each mentee with an alumni mentor aligned to their goals and industry.

3

Meet & Engage

Connect regularly — at least twice a month — through one-on-ones, site visits, networking events, and shared goals.

4

Graduate & Give Back

Complete the program, celebrate your growth, and consider joining our mentor pool to pay it forward.

Who Can Participate?

The program is open to UP students, recent graduates, and UPAASF alumni members in the Bay Area.

Mentees

Students & Early-Career Graduates

  • ✓ Currently enrolled at any University of the Philippines campus, in their senior year
  • ✓ Committed to meeting with your mentor at least once a month for the six-month cycle
  • ✓ Clear goals and a genuine willingness to learn, grow, and receive guidance

Mentors

Alumni Professionals & UPAASF Members

  • ✓ UP alumnus with at least 5 years of professional experience in your field
  • ✓ Current UPAASF member in good standing (or willing to join upon acceptance)
  • ✓ Able to dedicate approximately 2–4 hours per month to mentoring activities
  • ✓ Passionate about giving back to the UP community and developing the next generation
  • ✓ Open to attending at least one UPAASF program event per cycle alongside your mentee

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from prospective mentors and mentees.

When does the next cohort open for applications? +
Applications for each cohort open approximately 6–8 weeks before the cycle start date. The annual cohort opens in mid-August. Sign up for our newsletter or follow our social media channels to be notified when applications go live.
Is the program open to non-UPAASF members? +
Mentees who are currently enrolled UP students may apply regardless of membership. Mentor applicants are required to be — or become — UPAASF members.
What industries are represented in the mentor pool? +
Our mentors represent industries in finance, healthcare, public policy, technology, entrepreneurship, and academia. We continue to grow our mentor base, so if your target industry is not yet covered, please reach out and we'll do our best to find a suitable match.
Can mentoring meetings be conducted virtually? +
Absolutely. Mentor-mentee pairs are free to meet in-person, virtually via Zoom or Google Meet, or a combination of both. Flexibility is built into the program structure to accommodate busy schedules and varying locations across the Bay Area.
What happens if the pairing isn't a good fit? +
We take matching seriously, but we understand that not every pairing will be a perfect fit. If a mentor or mentee feels the relationship isn't working after a reasonable good-faith effort, they can request a reassignment by contacting the program coordinator. We handle these situations with care and confidentiality.
Is there a fee to participate? +
There is no fee to participate as a mentee or mentor. The program is made possible through UPAASF membership dues and the generous volunteer time of our alumni community. We simply ask participants to honor their commitments and contribute positively to the program.
